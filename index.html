Project Idea

Overview

A future project I would love to do would be to make a "robotic tabletop pet", only around 3*3 inches in size it will roam around the consumers table acting as a small companion. This robot will show varieties of emotions and display affection to ensure the user has a sense of happiness in the presence of this "pet". I also intend on making the robot able to interact with its surrounding and recharge itself on a home station like a "Roomba".

Motivation

Many people in tiny apartments in major cities and even smaller suburbs and city towns frequently live alone. Being alone too often takes a major toll on mental health, and these people live in areas who can not get any pets or companions due to rental rules, in particular university students in private housing in a recent study from HESA indicated that there was a 210% increase in the number of students who left university as a result of mental health issues between 2010 and 2015. To combat these numbers even slightly, I want to make this little robot to replenish some happiness in people and students left alone.

Description

  • Features of this robot include various emotions such as happiness, sadness, anger, hunger and love to enable those who own the robot to feel as though it is as close to reality as possible to replace the need for a physical pet due to the absence of the ability to own a pet without the added responsibility of having an actual animal to care for and have the repercussions for illegally housing an animal against a landlords rules.

  • A major design aspect is the pincer like appendages to allow for the ability to grab on to objects like a small crab and move it around as it pleases or the owner pleases as to add some entertainment and games, with the added possibility to pose for pictures.

  • The robot will be connected via Bluetooth to an android or iPhone device with an external application that doubles as a controller.

  • Another feature is a full LCD display which emulates the different emotions the robot is programmed to feel and show.

  • The robot will have a rechargeable home base to which it retrieves t whenever it gets less than 10% on battery to give the impression that it never needs to be manually "recharged" like an object.

  • The robot is able to listen to vocal commands for things such as showing the weather, playing games of fetch and paper scissors rock (with the app) so the robot will have some personality and the user will be able to get some enjoyment out of the robot so it is not just decoration.

  • Can display weather and information on the LCD screen as well as showing different hand symbols for paper scissors rock which is randomly generated through code on the app, when you lose the game the robot will show party poppers and confetti and when you win the game the robot will show a storm on the screen.

  • The robot will have 6 wheels similar to the "Curiosity Rover" to enable full rotational movement and the ability to climb on "rocky" terrains to give the illusion that it is a real conscious being.

  • The robot will have built in obstacle detection, so it is able to move around to prevent running off tables, bumping into walls or other like objects. If the robot does encounter an object it should be able to move around it or if possible move that object. It is also intended that if the robot does something it cannot remove itself from it will show dissatisfaction to ask for help.

  • The final feature I intend to have in this robot is the ability to play fetch, with a QR cube, it will be able to scan for the QR code and chase if thrown, pick up, or push the cube around at random.

Tools and Technologies

I will need Raspberry Pi technology using the Python programming language, as I believe It is most appropriate for a project like this one, as the Raspberry Pi computer has built in Bluetooth and Wi-Fi adaptors to create the software necessary to run the robot in the way I have envisioned it. I will also be needing a LCD screen itself for the display of the expressions and various interactive features. I will also be needing a soldering iron and solder with various other electronic components including, but not limited to, resistors, capacitors, LED's and diodes. I will also need an ultrasonic proximity sensor for the robot to be able to detect obstacles and move around them.

If this project succeeds at what I am intending to overcome, those who struggle with loneliness will be able to get some comfort and companionship out of the robot to reduce the dropout rate of universities and lower the mental illness rates throughout the world.
